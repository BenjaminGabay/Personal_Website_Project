I am still open minded as to what field or specification of computer science I want to go into. A main interest of mine though has always been artificial intelligence and machine learning. So, my goals would be to do something with those.

To gain some exposure to machine learning, I am involved in a research group with several other undergraduate and graduate students working with Dr. Sharon at Texas A&M. All the projects involve the use of machine learning in some way. Currently, my research team is designing and building several tracks to test different driving conditions. We then drive a small robotic vehicle equipped with a single centered camera around the track to collect data of proper driving. Finally, we use this data to build a neural network through imitation learning to see if the vehicle can then navigate the track on its own. In the future I hope to expand this project to include reinforced learning techniques so that prior data collection is not used, and the car will learn in real-time. The research lab website and list of all the projects being worked on can be found here.
